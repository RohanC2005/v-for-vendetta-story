THE CHOICE

Curfew has already begun.

Streetlights cast long reflections across wet pavement. Cameras swivel quietly on metal poles, scanning empty roads.

Evey Hammond hurries down a narrow alley, hoping to reach home before another patrol notices her.

Three Fingermen step into the path.

Their tone is casual at first. A curfew violation. Routine questioning.

But the moment stretches.

Their laughter grows louder.

Their authority begins to feel less official and more predatory.

Evey backs against the brick wall.

At the far end of the alley, unseen in the darkness, you watch.

The mask rests against your face. The knives rest in your sleeves.

You were not planning to reveal yourself tonight.

But history rarely asks permission.

A — Intervene. Step into the alley and stop the Fingermen. B — Stay hidden. Observe what happens instead of interfering.
