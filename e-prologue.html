PROLOGUE

London has learned how to survive.

The Norsefire regime controls every broadcast, every newspaper, every voice that rises too loudly.

Surveillance towers watch the streets. Curfew sirens echo through the night.

Most people no longer question it.

They work. They return home before dark. They keep their heads down.

Evey Hammond tries to live the same way.

But tonight she is walking home after curfew.

The streets are empty. The rain hides the sound of approaching footsteps.

Sometimes in a controlled city, one small moment is enough to change everything.
